# Quantum Mechanics

## 1. Principles

Quantum mechanics governs microscopic systems with probabilistic laws.  
Its postulates establish states, observables, and measurement outcomes.

---

## 2. State Vectors and Observables

### 2.1 Hilbert Space
States are vectors $|\psi\rangle$ in a Hilbert space.  

### 2.2 Observables
Represented by Hermitian operators $\hat O$ with eigenvalues as possible outcomes.

---

## 3. Schrödinger Equation

### 3.1 Time-Dependent Form
$$
i\hbar \frac{\partial}{\partial t} |\psi(t)\rangle = \hat H |\psi(t)\rangle.
$$

### 3.2 Time-Independent Form
$$
\hat H |\psi\rangle = E |\psi\rangle.
$$

---

## 4. Wave Mechanics

### 4.1 Position Representation
Wavefunction $\psi(\vec r, t) = \langle \vec r | \psi(t)\rangle$.  
Probability density:
$$
P(\vec r, t) = |\psi(\vec r, t)|^2.
$$

### 4.2 Operators
- Momentum: $\hat p = -i\hbar \nabla$.  
- Position: multiplication by $x$.  
- Commutation: $[\hat x, \hat p] = i\hbar$.

---

## 5. Fundamental Systems

### 5.1 Free Particle
Plane-wave solutions:
$$
\psi_k(x,t) = e^{i(kx - \omega t)}, \quad E = \frac{\hbar^2 k^2}{2m}.
$$

### 5.2 Harmonic Oscillator
Hamiltonian:
$$
\hat H = \frac{\hat p^2}{2m} + \tfrac{1}{2} m \omega^2 \hat x^2.
$$
Energy levels:
$$
E_n = \hbar \omega \left(n + \tfrac{1}{2}\right).
$$

### 5.3 Hydrogen Atom
Radial Schrödinger equation yields energy spectrum:
$$
E_n = -\frac{13.6 \, \text{eV}}{n^2}.
$$

---

## 6. Measurement and Uncertainty

### 6.1 Born Rule
Probability of outcome $a$:
$$
P(a) = |\langle a | \psi \rangle|^2.
$$

### 6.2 Uncertainty Principle
$$
\Delta x \, \Delta p \geq \frac{\hbar}{2}.
$$

---

## 7. Quantum Dynamics

- Superposition: $|\psi\rangle = c_1 |\phi_1\rangle + c_2 |\phi_2\rangle$.  
- Entanglement: non-factorizable states with nonlocal correlations.  
- Spin: intrinsic angular momentum, $S = \hbar/2$ for electrons.

---

## 8. Summary
Quantum mechanics provides a probabilistic framework describing microscopic matter, leading naturally into **quantum field theory**.
