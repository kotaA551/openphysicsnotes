# Classical Electromagnetism

## 1. Foundations

Electromagnetism unifies the phenomena of electricity and magnetism into a single theoretical framework, elegantly expressed through **Maxwell’s equations**.  
The theory explains how electric charges produce electric fields, how currents and changing electric fields produce magnetic fields, and how electromagnetic waves propagate through space.

---

## 2. Electrostatics

### 2.1 Coulomb’s Law
The force between two point charges $q_1$ and $q_2$ separated by vector $\vec r$:
$$
\vec F = \frac{1}{4 \pi \varepsilon_0} \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2} \hat r.
$$

### 2.2 Electric Field
Definition of the electric field as force per unit charge:
$$
\vec E(\vec r) = \frac{\vec F}{q}.
$$

For a point charge $q$:
$$
\vec E(\vec r) = \frac{1}{4 \pi \varepsilon_0} \frac{q}{r^2} \hat r.
$$

### 2.3 Gauss’s Law
Electric flux through a closed surface equals enclosed charge divided by $\varepsilon_0$:
$$
\oint_S \vec E \cdot d\vec A = \frac{Q_\text{enc}}{\varepsilon_0}.
$$

---

## 3. Magnetostatics

### 3.1 Biot–Savart Law
Magnetic field produced by a steady current $I$ along wire element $d\vec l$:
$$
d\vec B = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{I \, d\vec l \times \hat r}{r^2}.
$$

### 3.2 Ampère’s Law
For steady currents:
$$
\oint_C \vec B \cdot d\vec l = \mu_0 I_\text{enc}.
$$

---

## 4. Maxwell’s Equations (Differential Form)

1. Gauss’s Law for Electricity  
   $$
   \nabla \cdot \vec E = \frac{\rho}{\varepsilon_0}.
   $$

2. Gauss’s Law for Magnetism  
   $$
   \nabla \cdot \vec B = 0.
   $$

3. Faraday’s Law of Induction  
   $$
   \nabla \times \vec E = - \frac{\partial \vec B}{\partial t}.
   $$

4. Ampère–Maxwell Law  
   $$
   \nabla \times \vec B = \mu_0 \vec J + \mu_0 \varepsilon_0 \frac{\partial \vec E}{\partial t}.
   $$

---

## 5. Electromagnetic Waves

From Maxwell’s equations in vacuum:
$$
\nabla^2 \vec E - \mu_0 \varepsilon_0 \frac{\partial^2 \vec E}{\partial t^2} = 0,
\quad
\nabla^2 \vec B - \mu_0 \varepsilon_0 \frac{\partial^2 \vec B}{\partial t^2} = 0.
$$

Speed of propagation:
$$
c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \varepsilon_0}}.
$$

Thus, light is identified as an electromagnetic wave.

---

## 6. Energy and Momentum in Fields

### 6.1 Energy Density
The electromagnetic energy density:
$$
u = \tfrac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2 + \tfrac{1}{2\mu_0} B^2.
$$

### 6.2 Poynting Vector
Energy flux (power per unit area) carried by the fields:
$$
\vec S = \frac{1}{\mu_0} \vec E \times \vec B.
$$

### 6.3 Momentum Density
Electromagnetic momentum density:
$$
\vec g = \varepsilon_0 \vec E \times \vec B.
$$

---

## 7. Potentials and Gauge Freedom

### 7.1 Scalar and Vector Potentials
Fields expressed as:
$$
\vec B = \nabla \times \vec A, \quad \vec E = - \nabla \phi - \frac{\partial \vec A}{\partial t}.
$$

### 7.2 Gauge Transformations
Invariance under:
$$
\vec A' = \vec A + \nabla \chi, \quad \phi' = \phi - \frac{\partial \chi}{\partial t}.
$$

---

## 8. Relativistic Formulation

### 8.1 Four-Potential
Combine potentials into:
$$
A^\mu = \left( \frac{\phi}{c}, \vec A \right).
$$

### 8.2 Electromagnetic Field Tensor
$$
F^{\mu\nu} = \partial^\mu A^\nu - \partial^\nu A^\mu.
$$

Maxwell’s equations reduce to compact tensor equations:
$$
\partial_\mu F^{\mu\nu} = \mu_0 J^\nu, 
\quad \partial_{[\alpha} F_{\beta\gamma]} = 0.
$$

---

## 9. Summary

Electromagnetism unifies electric and magnetic phenomena under a single field theory.  
From Coulomb’s law to Maxwell’s equations, it explains the structure of electromagnetic waves, energy transfer, and the relativistic invariance of field laws. This classical framework laid the foundation for both **special relativity** and **quantum electrodynamics**.
